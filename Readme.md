# Earthquake Impact Analyzer

## Research Questions
The main questions motivating our analysis are:

- Does a higher magnitude mean more deaths and damage, and is there a point where earthquakes start becoming truly deadly?
- Do earthquakes closer to the surface cause more harm than deeper ones at the same magnitude?
- Which regions get hit the hardest, and are some areas more vulnerable than others even when the earthquakes are similar in size?
- Has the human cost of earthquakes changed over time, or does it stay roughly the same year to year?
 


## Hypotheses
We expect to find that:

- Bigger earthquakes cause more damage. Seismic energy grows exponentially with magnitude, so we expect deaths and dollar losses to follow the same pattern.
- Shallower earthquakes are more destructive. Less distance for energy to travel means more shaking at the surface where people actually live.
- Some regions are more vulnerable than others. Two places can have the same size earthquake and very different outcomes depending on infrastructure and emergency response.
- Earthquakes don't happen randomly. We expect them to cluster along fault lines and plate boundaries rather than being spread evenly across the globe.
